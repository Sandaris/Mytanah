# Product

## Register

product

## Users

FYP examiners and demo audiences — academic evaluators encountering MyPropertyIQ cold, typically for the first time. They need to immediately grasp both what the product does and that it was built with genuine care. First impression is decisive; polish and clarity matter far more than feature breadth. A secondary audience is Malaysian property buyers and investors who might use the tool to support an actual decision.

## Product Purpose

MyPropertyIQ is a Malaysian property intelligence dashboard that unifies AI-powered valuation, market cycle analysis, ROI calculation, and a sentiment index into one coherent tool. It exists to demonstrate that complex property data science can be made legible and actionable — and to show the FYP panel that the underlying models have been wrapped in a production-quality interface, not just a notebook. Success means an examiner leaves understanding both what the tool does and why the design choices support it.

## Brand Personality

Warm, trustworthy, authoritative. Think knowledgeable financial advisor, not sterile analytics platform. Approachable and clear — never intimidating. The palette (cream + forest green + earth brown) carries warmth; the typography pairing (Cormorant Garamond display + DM Sans UI) carries editorial authority. The voice is confident, grounded, and human.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS / admin dashboards** — dark sidebars, blue-accent card grids, table-heavy layouts. The corporate-analytics-tool aesthetic that makes every dashboard look like the same product.
- **Government / academic reports** — dense, PDF-aesthetic, dry. Bureaucratic information presentation that signals "this was built to fulfil a requirement," not to serve a user.

## Design Principles

1. **Data earns its place** — every chart and number answers a question a user might have; context always accompanies the figure. No data displayed without a label, unit, and brief interpretive frame.
2. **Clarity earns trust** — if an examiner has to think twice about what a number means, the design has failed. Labels, empty states, and loading moments all matter.
3. **Warmth through craft** — the palette and typography carry emotional warmth. Interactions should feel considered, not just functional: hover states, transitions, and micro-details signal care.
4. **Confidence without clutter** — restraint signals mastery. A polished, reduced interface that shows exactly what's needed says more about the work behind it than a dense feature dump.
5. **First impression is the product** — this is a demo-first tool. The intro sequence, the first visible page, and the onboarding flow must land immediately; the examiner's opinion is largely formed in the first 30 seconds.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA as the baseline. English-language UI. Reduced-motion support for all transitions (the intro globe sequence in particular should have a `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback). Contrast ratios verified for the cream-surface / forest-green text combination. No color-only encoding of signal states (up/stable/down indicators should include iconography or text, not just hue).
